Limits

Overview

Maintainer: Interval

Limits are like the laws of everything; they are to always be followed.

Since the laws of physics don't exist anywhere, we have these instead.

Limit 1

Summary: Nothing is more certain than change.

To be more precist, absolutes quite often do not exist.

Things always change, they are not guaranteed to be the same.

Exceptions are few, this limit is an exception to itself, and the ODR is a known exception. But others should not exist.

Limit 2

Summary: Of you, there are 3. You, δ you, and φ you.

Each thing to exist has two counterparts, one in the hypercomplex space, and the other in otherside.

If one dies, all of them die. And no, they don't just spontaneously combust.

It's convenient, that if one dies, both others will be in a situation of guaranteed death as well.

Hypercomplex characters are prefixed with "δ", otherside characters are prefixed with "φ".
